The LightSpeed Cinemas¶

1. Context¶

The management of a chain of cinemas called Light Speed cinemas are interested in reviewing the data for the 3 cinemas for the past 3 months. The data regards the total number of ticket sales they have made, along with the number of sales and revenue for the concession stand (Soda, Popcorn, and Candy). In particular, the management are interested in comparing the revenue of the concession stand between the three cinemas.

As a result of an interview with the management of Lightspeed Cinemas, the following meeting notes have been compiled in order to better understand the context:

  1. the management is not sure about which cinema is performing the best, but thinks that it might be the one that sells the products for the highest price;
  2. similarly, the management thinks that the most successful concession is the one that sold the most products;
  3. the cinemas don’t have any say on what movies get made, and how they’re advertised and how an audience will review them – they can only sell tickets to whatever movie is out. Whenever they sell a ticket, a large portion of it goes to the movie studio, then from what is left, once the cost of running the cinema has been considered, profit margins on ticket sales are slim. So the main revenue they generate is from the concession stand. Their objective is to maximise revenue at the concession stand for each ticket sold. In other words, they want to understand which cinema generates the most revenue per customer at the concession stand;
  4. they'd like to be able to clearly identify which cinema was the best, and what factors made it the best.

The objective of this report is to review the data and the context provided, and to highlight which of the three cinemas has the most successful concession stand, and how the others compare to it.

2. Resources¶

The data has been collected and organized in three small datasets, namely:

  • lightspeed-cinema-product-cost.csv: the price each of the cinemas charges for the items they sell at the concession stand;
  • lightspeed-month-cinema-product-sales-revenue.csv: the monthly sales number and revenue for each of the items each cinema sells at the concession stand;
  • lightspeed-month-cinema-tickets.csv: the number of tickets sold for each of the three cinemas of the three months.

3.5 Point 3: the total revenue generated and the total number of tickets sold¶

We also know that, according to the third point raised by the client, the revenue is a fundamental indicator of success. Let us see the total revenue generated by the cinemas during the last three months of activity. Although we know that ticket sales are not very relevant to the success of a cinema, we still might be interested in seeing how well the three cinemas fare in that regard.

Cinema 3 is selling the most, making the highest total revenue and selling the highest number of tickets. But, is it the most successful?

3.6 Point 4: the average revenue per ticket sold¶

If we read through the meeting notes, we should notice that the actual measure of success is the revenue per ticket. This is because the objective of LightSpeed is to maximise the amount of revenue it makes from the concession stand for each consumer that comes in, since the profit margin for tickets is extremely thin.

The first graph (the one about the highest price), coupled with the graph below (created by dividing the total revenue by the total number of tickets), tells a quite different story.

In [6]:
rev_per_ticket = pd.merge(total_rev, total_tickets, on="cinema")

def get_revPerTicket(datafr):
    datafr['revPerTicket'] = datafr['salesRev'] / datafr['tickets']
    return datafr

rev_per_ticket = rev_per_ticket.groupby(['cinema']).apply(get_revPerTicket)

f, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(15, 8))
ax.set_title('Cinema 2 has the highest revenue per ticket', fontsize=20)

g=sns.barplot(x="cinema", y="revPerTicket",
            palette="Blues", edgecolor=".6",
            data=rev_per_ticket)

On one hand, Cinema 1 and Cinema 3 generate almost the same amount of revenue per customer. On the other, Cinema 2 generates much more value per customer. Based on our client's indications, we can say that Cinema 2 is therefore the best performing cinema in terms of revenue generated per customer. This is due to the fact it charges the highest prices for its products.
